Efficient AI

Our research in Efficient AI focuses on developing next-generation techniques to optimize artificial intelligence systems across their entire lifecycle. We pioneer novel approaches in training optimization, inference acceleration (including pruning, quantization, and compression), and neural architecture design. Our work enables AI models to achieve state-of-the-art performance with significantly improved computational efficiency, reduced energy consumption, and minimized memory footprint - making advanced AI accessible for resource-constrained environments.

Embodied AI

The Embodied AI research at our lab investigates intelligent systems that perceive, reason, and interact with physical environments. We develop algorithms that bridge the gap between virtual intelligence and real-world embodiment, focusing on multimodal perception, physical interaction learning, and autonomous decision-making in dynamic settings. Our work spans robotic learning, simulated-to-real transfer, and the development of AI agents capable of adaptive behavior in complex, unstructured environments.
